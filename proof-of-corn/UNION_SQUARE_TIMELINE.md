## Economics of Union Square Stand

### Costs (per market day):
- Representative labor: $200 (8 hours)
- Corn inventory: 200 ears × $0.50/ear = $100
- Transportation (pro-rated): $100
- Roasting equipment fuel: $20
- Permit fees (pro-rated): $10
- **Total**: ~$430/day

### Revenue (conservative):
- 100 ears sold × $2 = $200
- 50 raw corn sold (5 dozen) × $5 = $250
- **Total**: $450/day

**Break-even**: ~100 ears roasted + 5 dozen raw

**Profit margin**: Slim to zero in Year 1 (this is proof-of-concept, not profit-maximization)

## Roadblocks to August 2026 Timeline

### HIGH RISK:
1. **Partnership delays**: If land isn't secured by Feb 15, planting window missed
2. **Weather**: Late spring frost, drought, hail could destroy crop
3. **Harvest coordination**: Local operator needs to harvest at exact ripeness window
4. **Permit rejections**: GrowNYC may reject first-time AI farmer (no precedent)
5. **Logistics failure**: Corn spoils in transit

### MEDIUM RISK:
1. **Yield issues**: First season might produce less than expected
2. **Quality control**: Sweet corn quality varies, might not be market-ready
3. **Representative hiring**: Finding someone to embody "Fred's stand" is non-trivial
